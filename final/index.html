About Me

Montakan Namthong–Graphic Designer

I am currently a third year student in the Graphic Designer program at Massachusetts College of Art and Design, visual designer who loves puppy, Blythe Dolls, bags and hats. I love almost everything about technology; it is intended to make our lives easier.

I really like to learn and know something new, I think being Designer, though, is really a skill that is taught and learned. It is taken me down different paths that I would have never explored before. A lot of times what really excites me most about creativity is the fact that I never really know what I am going to have to create. So it is always that challenge of having to do something that I have not done before. This is a great way to develop skills, learn more about visual concepts to communicate ideas that inspire, inform, or captivate audience. And all of that enables me to see more options in front of my eyes.

Over the years my work and design style has become contemporary, artistic and organic. I love to work with watercolor, textures and patterns, and creating a beautiful that is unique to each work.

Editorial Design

A Narrative Sequence

Based a selection of texts provided, a sequence of pages, comprising a short story or an essay, were designed using type and images formatted as a contemporary magazine. The project includes use of page grids, to build a series of 2–page spreads, using design methods to promote readers’ understanding and appreciation.

Posters & Postage Stamps

Series

The objective of this assignment is to convey a message that raises awareness and motivates change, concern– ing sustainability, using topics of water, trees or endangered species. A set of closely-related variant postage stamps and a poster, which includes a persuasive fact, were designed.

Farmers Market

Advocacy Poster Series

The assignment is to design a series of 3 posters to promote the weekly Farmers’ Market through the context of encouraging ‘better eating for better health’. The series of 3 posters will be used as an awareness campaign on public transportation systems (in and on subways and buses, bus shelters) and on free-standing street kiosks and possibly banners in the Mission Hill neighborhood and the greater Boston area.
